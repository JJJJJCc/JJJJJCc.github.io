Augmented Reality Enabled Smart Fidget for Supporting Just-in-Time Emotion Regulation Using Machine Learning


Support Just-in-Time Emotion Regulation (Ongoing)


With the rapid evolution of soft-bodied sensing technologies and small processors that can be embedded in tangibles, and the recent explosion of deep learning techniques and tools, we are now in a position to develop a new class of interaction device that can capture and interpret affective and attention regulation-related qualities of touch. The goal of this project is to innovate a novel platform for the support of just-in-time emotional regulation, using a combination of a smart fidget (tangible user interface), Augmented Reality glasses, and machine learning to detect patterns of emotional state change related to dget behavior, as shown in Figure 1. When using the system, the user picks up the dget ball, which is tethered to a laptop and manipulates it for 10 seconds. Then the machine learning algorithm (developed by a research team at UCSC [1] ) will detect the user‚Äôs aective state with the input of pressure data vector and output an aective vector that predicts the possibility of positive emotion, negative emotion, sadness, anger, and anxiety. Then, the AR glasses will receive the emotion detection result from the laptop and trigger the corresponding guided AR self-regulation journey (this includes visualization/music patterns and dget manipulation guidance) to help users try to get into the desired aective state. In the end, the user‚Äôs aective state will be re-detected by the machine learning system to show whether the user moves to a better emotional state than the starting point.

Early Prototype Stage


I am currently designing the visualization of AR emotion-regulation journeys from ideation to storyboarding, prototyping, implementation, user testing, and iteration.


Fire


To visualize the initial affective state as ‚Äúangry‚Äù, we find the metaphor of angry is fire. Intuitively, angry should be released in various forms: some people run, shout, throw stuff, fight, etc. In this case, we can guide users to squeeze or punch the ball for ‚Äúenergy releasing‚Äù. We design the raining effect as the feedback for users‚Äô squeezing action. While squeezing the fidget balls for an amount of time, the fire will gradually become smaller until it is extinguished. After the fire disappeared, we designed a blooming scene to visualize the better affective state. The following Figure 2a illustrates the walkthrough of this lens.




Lotus


Buddism culture uses ‚Äúlotus‚Äù as the metaphor of our body and emotion, and some mindful guidance pointed out imagining lotus‚Äôs opening and closing when breathing and walking helps calm down our body and emotion. Inspired by the concept of ‚Äúlotus‚Äù, we designed the interaction that our fidgeting behavior controls the openness and the closeness of the lotus to move the user‚Äôs anxious state into a calm state. As shown in Figure 2b, the AR experience will guide the user to squeeze the ball in a regular pattern; if following the pattern the lotus will close and open with a regular pattern. In the end, the lotus will start to open and close automatically and glow, which stands for the better state the user is moving to.




Bubble


In daily life, poping the bubble seems to be a fulfilling and relaxing experience. In our design, the user can pop the random bubbles, while new bubbles with different colors will show up. In the end, bubbles in all colors will be floating the air.
