Cooking is more than a mundane daily task for filling ourselves, and it intertwines with creativity, relaxation, and social connection. Still, many people cannot feel these pleasant experiences due to the diculty and boredom of the cooking process. Previous Human-Food Interaction (HFI) research demonstrated several instrumental cooking applications with Augmented Reality, thermal sensors, and motion tracking to help people follow the recipe precisely. Most of them focus on eciency instead of fun and creativity, motivating people to enjoy cooking in the long run. This project aims to 1) value introducing playfulness over classical gamication approaches for learning productivity and 2) create a new cooking experience for people who do not regard cooking as something they enjoy and possibly ignites their interests in cooking. This project will use game- or play-inspired mechanisms to explore how playfulness can add value to the cooking experience. Rather than lead them into a pure virtual game environment, we hope cooking is the main activity people experience. Using AR glasses seems to be a good choice that both keeps the real-life cooking experience and provides augmented visual eects, sounds, and interactions.

Many people understand recipes as something to follow precisely whereas more experienced cooks more and more use recipes as inspiration. People who regard cooking as something they enjoy show their love for ‚Äúfree-form‚Äù cooking. In other words, after learning fundamental cooking techniques, they can express creativity by customizing the standard recipe with their own preferences. Dierent from the standard recipe applications, we will design the experience with these three strategies:
1) Lead players to experiment with the freedom to bring them closer to self-guided cooking. ‚Ä®
2) Add meta-rules of cooking (Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat) to liberate the cook from instructions. ‚Ä®
3) Integrate more domain knowledge. ‚Ä®
